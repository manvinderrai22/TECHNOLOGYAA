Understanding Semiconductors

What is a Semiconductor?

A semiconductor is a substance, usually a solid chemical element or compound, that has a specific electrical resistivity. It is neither a full conductor (like copper) nor an insulator (like rubber). Its conductivity lies between these two extremes.

The Silicon Base

Silicon is the core of the modern electronics industry. By doping silicon with other elements (like Boron or Phosphorus), we create "N-type" or "P-type" silicon, which allows electricity to flow in a controlled way, acting as a switch.

Applications

From smartphones and laptops to EVs and AI servers, semiconductors are the "brain". India is now pushing heavily into manufacturing these chips to power the next generation of technology.
